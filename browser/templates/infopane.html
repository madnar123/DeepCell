The right pane of Caliban displays an image of cells. This can be toggled between the raw image (not shown) and the cell segmentation masks for that image (shown). Each cell value is represented by a different color. 

The left pane of Caliban displays information. The frame number you are viewing will always be displayed in the top left corner. When you mouse over a cell in the right pane, the tracking information for that cell will be displayed in the bottom left corner. Cell selections and confirmations will also be displayed in the bottom left corner.

Your Task

Your job is to go through each frame and make sure that cell lineages and divisions are correct. As you go through each frame, you will notice sometimes that individual cells will change color, two cells masks are identified as with one cell label, along with other mistakes. You will use keyboard operations in order to fix this and ensure that each cell has a unique individual label that is maintained across the frames.

The Keyboard Tools

Mouse over a cell mask to see cell label and lineage information.

Click on a cell mask to select it.

esc | Cancel operation; do this if you click on a cell(s) and want to undo your click.

z | Switch between annotations and raw images; use this to check if your masks and labels match up to the actual cell image

Scroll Wheel | In raw image mode, scroll up/down to change the brightness of the image. 

h | Highlight mode; if used, this will highlight the cells that are selected with a red mask.

To navigate through frames:

a | Go back to previous frame

d | Go forward to next frame

Single-Click Edit Operations:

c | create: click on one cell mask (cell C). Hit “c” to create a new cell track starting from that frame. 

x | delete: click on one cell mask (cell A). Hit "x" to delete the mask. This is useful when you have a couple pixels in a frame that represent a cell, but you want to remove that label.

Two-Click Edit Operations:

r | replace: click on one cell mask (cell A) then another (cell B). Hit “r” to replace all instances of cell B with cell A.

p | parent: click on one cell mask (cell D) then another (cell E). Hit “p” to assign cell D as the parent of cell E. Cell D will now include cell E in its list of daughter cells, and cell E will list cell D as its parent.

s | swap: click on one cell mask (cell F) then another (cell G). Hit “s” to swap the cell values (does not swap parent/daughter information). This affects all frames of the movie.

w | watershed: this function is to separate one cell mask (cell H) into two (cell H and cell I). Click on one pixel in a cell mask (cell H), then another pixel in the same cell mask. These points should correspond to the centers of the cells you are trying to separate. You can select these positions while you are looking at the raw channel. The first click should be on the cell that you want to remain cell H.  Watershed (currently) does not create a cell track in the same way that “create” does. Do not watershed twice in a row without replacing the watershed cell with an existing cell track first (some exceptions exist).

Caliban currently doesn’t support an option to reverse an operation, so if a watershed operation is not fixable, you will have to refresh the browser page and start over.
